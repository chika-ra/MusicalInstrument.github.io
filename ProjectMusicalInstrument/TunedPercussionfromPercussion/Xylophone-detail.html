Xylophone

The xylophone is a tuned percussion instrument made up of wooden bars of varying lengths that produce musical notes when struck with mallets. It is known for its bright, clear sound.

Key Features:

  • Uses wooden bars arranged in the pattern of a piano keyboard
  • Played using one or two mallets
  • Produces a sharp, melodic tone
  • Commonly used in orchestras, marching bands, and music education

Famous Xylophone Performers:

  • George Hamilton Green
  • Bob Becker
  • Evelyn Glennie
