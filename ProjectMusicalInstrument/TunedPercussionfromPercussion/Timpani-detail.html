Timpani

The timpani, or kettledrums, are large, tunable drums played with mallets. They produce deep, resonant tones and are a fundamental part of the symphony orchestra.

Key Features:

  • Large copper or fiberglass bowls
  • Played with felt-tipped mallets
  • Pitch can be adjusted with a pedal
  • Used for rhythm and dramatic effect

Used In:

  • Orchestral and symphonic music
  • Film scores
  • Classical compositions
