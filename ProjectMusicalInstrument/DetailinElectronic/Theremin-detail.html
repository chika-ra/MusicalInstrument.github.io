Theremin

The theremin is one of the earliest electronic instruments, played without physical contact. It produces eerie, oscillating sounds and is famous in sci-fi soundtracks and experimental music.

Key Features:

  • Played by moving hands near two antennas
  • No physical touch needed
  • Unique, haunting sound quality
  • Used in film scores, avant-garde, and ambient music

Famous Thereminists:

  • Clara Rockmore
  • Lydia Kavina
  • Jean-Michel Jarre
