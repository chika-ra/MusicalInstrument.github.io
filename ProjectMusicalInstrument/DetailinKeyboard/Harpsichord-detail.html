Harpsichord

The harpsichord is a keyboard instrument that was widely used during the Renaissance and Baroque periods. Unlike the piano, it plucks strings rather than striking them, producing a distinctive, bright sound.

Key Features:

  • Strings are plucked by quills when keys are pressed
  • Used primarily in Baroque music
  • Often has multiple keyboards (manuals)
  • Limited dynamic control compared to piano
