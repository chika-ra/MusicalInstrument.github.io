Oboe

The oboe is a woodwind instrument with a double reed and a conical bore, known for its penetrating, bright, and slightly nasal tone. It is a prominent melodic instrument in orchestras, wind ensembles, and chamber music.

Key Features:

  • Uses a double reed
  • Conical bore for expressive, focused tone
  • Often used to give the tuning note ("A") in orchestras
  • Highly expressive in solo and ensemble settings
