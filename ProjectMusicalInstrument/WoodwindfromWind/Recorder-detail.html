Recorder

The recorder is a woodwind instrument that dates back to the medieval and baroque periods. It is known for its simple design, sweet tone, and educational value, often being the first wind instrument taught to beginners.

Key Features:

  • End-blown flute with whistle mouthpiece
  • Commonly made of plastic or wood
  • Available in multiple sizes (soprano, alto, tenor, bass)
  • Used in early music, education, and ensemble playing
