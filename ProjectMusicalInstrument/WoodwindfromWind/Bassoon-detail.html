Bassoon

The bassoon is a large woodwind instrument with a double reed and a long wooden body. It is known for its warm, rich, and reedy tone, often used for bass lines and comedic or expressive character in orchestral music.

Key Features:

  • Uses a double reed
  • Has a long body folded in half to make it more compact
  • Capable of wide pitch and dynamic range
  • Essential in orchestras and chamber music for bass voice

Famous Bassoon Players:

  • Klaus Thunemann
  • Sergio Azzolini
  • Kim Walker
