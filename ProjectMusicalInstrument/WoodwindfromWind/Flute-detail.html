Flute

The flute is a woodwind instrument that produces sound from the flow of air across an opening. Unlike other woodwinds, it does not use a reed. The flute is known for its bright, clear, and pure tone, often heard in orchestras, bands, and solo performances.

Key Features:

  • Played by blowing air across the mouthpiece hole
  • Made of metal (silver, gold, or nickel)
  • Lightweight and agile in fast passages
  • Wide range and expressive dynamics

Famous Flute Players:

  • Jean-Pierre Rampal
  • James Galway
  • Emmanuel Pahud
