Tambourine

The tambourine is a small hand-held percussion instrument that combines a drumhead with pairs of small metal jingles called "zils". It is widely used in many musical styles for rhythm and accent.

Key Features:

  • Consists of a frame, often with a drumhead
  • Metal jingles produce bright, ringing sounds
  • Played by shaking, hitting, or tapping
  • Common in folk, classical, pop, and world music

Used By:

  • Folk musicians
  • Orchestras and choirs
  • Pop and rock bands
