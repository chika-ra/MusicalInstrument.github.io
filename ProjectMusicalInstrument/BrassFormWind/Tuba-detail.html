Tuba

The tuba is the largest and lowest-pitched brass instrument. It provides the bass foundation in orchestras, concert bands, and brass ensembles. Despite its size, the tuba is capable of playing smooth melodic lines as well as powerful harmonies.

Key Features:

  • Lowest brass instrument in pitch
  • Played using a large cup-shaped mouthpiece
  • Typically has 3 to 6 valves
  • Provides bass support in ensembles
