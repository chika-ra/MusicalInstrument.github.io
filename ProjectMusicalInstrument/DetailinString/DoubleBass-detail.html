Double Bass

The double bass is the largest and lowest-pitched string instrument in the orchestra. It provides the foundation of harmony and rhythm.

Key Facts:

  • Stands over 6 feet tall
  • Has 4 strings, sometimes 5
  • Played with a bow or plucked (jazz style)
  • Used in orchestral, jazz, and rockabilly

Famous Double Bassists:

  • Charles Mingus
  • Edgar Meyer
  • Ray Brown
