Cello

The cello is a bowed string instrument with a rich, deep tone. It is larger than a violin and plays an essential role in orchestras and chamber music.

Key Facts:

  • Originated in the 16th century
  • Has four strings tuned in fifths
  • Played seated, with the instrument resting on the floor
  • Used in classical, contemporary, and film music
